Our Story

Why I Built Qura

Hi, I'm Harry. Like many of you reading this, I struggled with porn addiction for years. It cost me my mental health, relationships, and sense of self-worth. This is the app I wish I'd had during my darkest moments.

My Journey to Recovery

The Struggle

It started innocently enough in my teens - curiosity, boredom, easy access. But by my early twenties, porn had become my default coping mechanism for stress, loneliness, and anxiety. Every time I felt uncomfortable, I'd escape into pixels on a screen.

The consequences crept up slowly. I struggled with depression. My relationships suffered - intimacy became difficult, connection felt impossible. I isolated myself, convinced I was broken beyond repair. The shame was overwhelming, yet I couldn't stop.

I tried everything: willpower alone (failed repeatedly), generic habit trackers (too impersonal), porn blockers (easily bypassed), even therapy (helpful but expensive and not always available when I needed it most). Nothing addressed the core issue - I needed tools for the hardest moments AND a framework for long-term recovery.

Finding What Works

My breakthrough came when I stopped relying on willpower and started using a system. I discovered that recovery required multiple tools working together:

  • Tracking: Measuring my progress made the invisible visible
  • Breathing exercises: Calmed my nervous system during urges
  • Meditation: Taught me to observe urges without acting
  • Wisdom literature: Stoic philosophy gave me discipline
  • Emergency interventions: Real strategies saved me countless times

Most importantly, I learned to treat myself with compassion instead of punishment. Recovery isn't linear. There were setbacks, but each one taught me something.

The Transformation

Today, I'm not the same person who started this journey. The depression lifted. My relationships deepened. I found genuine confidence and self-respect that no amount of success or achievement had given me before.

But more than personal freedom, I gained a mission: to help others find the same path. Too many men are suffering in silence, convinced they're uniquely broken, trying to fight with inadequate tools. They deserve better.
